General Design

A look at the high-level design process, including trade-offs, rationale, and system-level diagrams. From concept to reality!

Design Overview

Project Goals

Our primary objective was to create a wall-climbing robot capable of drawing on vertical surfaces. This required balancing several key constraints: weight, power consumption, adhesion strength, and drawing precision.

Design Philosophy

We approached this project with a focus on modularity and iterative development. Each subsystem was designed to be independently testable while integrating seamlessly with the overall system.

Suction vs. Magnetic Adhesion: We chose impeller-based suction for its versatility across different wall materials, though it required more power than magnetic alternatives.

Omniwheels vs. Standard Wheels: Omniwheels provided superior maneuverability for precise drawing, despite increased mechanical complexity.

Wireless vs. Tethered: Wireless control offered better user experience and demonstration value, though it added communication complexity.

Design Iterations

Throughout development, we went through multiple design iterations, each addressing specific challenges discovered during testing. Key improvements included optimizing the impeller design for better suction, refining the marker mechanism for consistent line quality, and enhancing the control algorithms for smoother movement.
